What is Music Therapy

Music therapy is a form of therapy that uses music to promote physical, emotional, cognitive, and social well-being. It is a holistic approach that is used to address the needs of individuals of all ages and with various health conditions. Music therapy is an evidence-based practice that is used in a variety of settings including hospitals, schools, mental health facilities, nursing homes, and private practice.

How Music Therapy Works

Music therapy works by using music as a tool to achieve therapeutic goals. A music therapist will assess the needs of the individual and design a treatment plan that utilizes various music interventions to address their specific needs. Music interventions may include singing, listening to music, creating music, and movement to music. Music therapy can be used to improve communication skills, increase relaxation, decrease anxiety and depression, improve physical rehabilitation, and improve overall well-being.

Benefits of Music Therapy

Music therapy offers a wide range of benefits for individuals of all ages and with various health conditions. Some of the key benefits of music therapy include:


  • Reducing stress and anxiety
  • Improving physical health
  • Increasing socialization and communication
  • Improving self-expression and self-esteem
  • Enhancing cognitive skills
  • Providing emotional support
